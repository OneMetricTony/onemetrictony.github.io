# 09 — Business Accounting

**Hours / weeks:** 120 hours over 6 weeks (4 hours/day × 5 days).

## Learning Outcomes

By completion, students will be able to:
1. Apply the accounting equation and double-entry bookkeeping to record transactions in journals and ledgers for Canadian sole proprietorships, partnerships, and private enterprises.
2. Prepare a trial balance, adjusting entries, and a full set of financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow) consistent with ASPE and an introductory awareness of IFRS.
3. Calculate, file, and remit GST/HST and source deductions in line with CRA requirements, including basic T4/T2125 awareness.
4. Operate at least one cloud bookkeeping platform (such as QuickBooks, Xero, or Sage — selected term-by-term) to complete a full bookkeeping cycle.
5. Use AI-assisted tools responsibly for categorization, OCR capture, and forecasting while maintaining CPA-aligned standards of human oversight and confidentiality.
6. Build a basic operating budget and apply cost accounting fundamentals (fixed/variable, contribution margin, break-even).

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Wk 1:** Accounting equation, debits/credits, the Canadian regulatory landscape (CPA Canada, ASPE vs. IFRS overview).
- **Wk 2:** Journals, subsidiary ledgers, posting, trial balance, adjusting/closing entries.
- **Wk 3:** Financial statements — Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Statement of Cash Flows under ASPE.
- **Wk 4:** Payroll (CPP, EI, income tax), source deductions, and GST/HST collection/remittance via CRA My Business Account.
- **Wk 5:** Cost accounting basics — job vs. process costing, CVP analysis, contribution margin.
- **Wk 6:** Budgeting, variance analysis, AI-assisted forecasting, capstone review.

## AI Integration

- AI bookkeeping/auto-categorization (such as QuickBooks AI, Xero "Just Ask", Docyt).
- Receipt/invoice OCR capture (such as Dext, Hubdoc, Veryfi) — discuss 95-99% accuracy and exception handling.
- AI cash-flow forecasting and scenario modelling (such as Fathom, Jirav, or a spreadsheet copilot).
- Anomaly/fraud detection on transaction streams (such as MindBridge — Canadian-built).
- AI tax research assistants for CRA interpretations (such as Blue J, or general LLMs with guardrails).

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Practice-Firm Full Cycle (Wk 2–3):** Open books for a fictional Markham retailer, post 60+ transactions, run trial balance, produce ASPE-compliant statements.
2. **GST/HST + Payroll Filing Simulation (Wk 4):** Compute and prepare a quarterly HST return and a bi-weekly payroll run using a CRA sandbox-style workbook.
3. **AI-Assisted Cash-Flow Forecast (Wk 6):** Import 12 months of transactions into an AI forecasting tool, critique outputs, and present a 90-day rolling forecast with documented human-review notes.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

30% practice-firm project · 20% HST/payroll lab · 25% AI forecast capstone · 15% midterm · 10% professionalism.
AI tools are permitted and encouraged for categorization, OCR, and analysis, but every AI output must be reviewed, footnoted with the tool used, and signed off by the student per CPA Canada/CPA Ontario guidance on human oversight, accuracy, and client-data confidentiality. Submitting unreviewed AI output is academic misconduct.

## Pre/co-requisites

Pre-req: Business Math & Excel Fundamentals. Co-req: Business Communications.

## Instructor Depth Required

CPA (CA, CGA, or CMA legacy) or equivalent with minimum 5 years Canadian bookkeeping/SMB practice experience; demonstrated fluency in at least two cloud accounting platforms and current AI bookkeeping tools; PCCA 2005-compliant credentials on file with the College.
