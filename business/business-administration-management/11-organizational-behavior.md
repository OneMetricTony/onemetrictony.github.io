# 11 — Organizational Behavior

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours / 4 weeks (~20 instructional hours/week incl. lab).

## Learning Outcomes

1. Analyze individual behavior drivers (personality, perception, motivation, emotion) and apply frameworks (Big Five, expectancy theory, JD-R) to workplace cases.
2. Diagnose team dynamics, conflict, and leadership styles in co-located, hybrid, and human-AI augmented configurations.
3. Evaluate organizational culture, structure, and power, including how generative AI reshapes workflow authority and decision rights.
4. Design a change-management plan for an AI tool rollout using a recognized model (ADKAR, Kotter, or ADOPT) with measurable adoption KPIs.
5. Apply ethical, inclusive, and PIPEDA-aware practices when AI is used to observe, coach, or evaluate employees.
6. Communicate OB recommendations through written reports and facilitated team discussions.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Wk 1 (Individual):** OB foundations; personality and perception; motivation theories; emotional labour and well-being in remote work.
- **Wk 2 (Group):** Team formation and norms; conflict and negotiation; leadership (transformational, servant, shared); psychological safety; decision-making biases.
- **Wk 3 (Organization):** Culture and subcultures; structure (functional, matrix, network); power and politics; organizational change models (Lewin, Kotter, ADKAR).
- **Wk 4 (Modern Workplace):** Hybrid/distributed team design; human-AI team handoffs and decision rights; AI-augmented manager workflows; change management for AI rollout; ethics, surveillance, and inclusion.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic, examples only)

- Sentiment analysis on internal comms or pulse surveys (e.g., a generic LLM summarizing anonymized Slack/Teams threads).
- AI-facilitated retrospectives that cluster themes and surface blind spots.
- Manager coaching agents that role-play feedback conversations.
- Culture surveys analyzed by AI for thematic and tone patterns across open-text responses.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Culture audit (group):** collect a mock anonymized survey, use any AI tool to perform thematic and sentiment analysis, present findings and three culture interventions.
2. **Change-management plan (individual):** given a case where a mid-size Markham firm is rolling out an AI assistant to its sales team, apply ADKAR or ADOPT, including stakeholder map, communications cadence, training, and resistance mitigation.
3. **Hybrid team charter (group):** design norms, meeting rhythms, and human-AI task split for a 6-person distributed team.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

Participation 10% · Project 1 (25%) · Project 2 (30%) · Reflective journal 10% · Final case exam 25%.
AI use is permitted and encouraged for brainstorming, summarizing, and drafting; students must
(a) disclose tools and prompts in an AI-Use Appendix,
(b) verify all factual claims,
(c) not submit AI-generated text as unedited final analysis.
Exam is closed-AI to assess individual mastery.

## Pre/co-requisites

Pre-req: Introduction to Business or equivalent. Co-req: Business Communications.

## Instructor Depth Required

Master's degree in OB, HR, I/O Psychology, or MBA with OB concentration; 3+ years applied experience leading teams or change initiatives; demonstrated familiarity with generative AI in workplace contexts.
