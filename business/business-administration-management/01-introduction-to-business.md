# 01 — Introduction to Business

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours over 4 weeks (~20 hrs/wk lecture+lab) — foundational module in the 960h/48wk Business Administration Management Diploma.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. Explain the structure of the Canadian business environment, including forms of ownership, key functional areas (finance, marketing, operations, HR), and the regulatory context relevant to Ontario SMEs.
2. Analyze a real Canadian company's value chain, target market, and competitive position using publicly available data, including web-scraped and AI-summarized sources.
3. Apply foundational marketing, SEO, and paid-media concepts (search intent, keyword research, funnel stages, CAC/LTV) to a small-business scenario.
4. Demonstrate ethical, responsible use of generative AI for business research, ideation, and writing — including disclosure, verification, and PIPEDA-aware data handling.
5. Communicate business analysis in writing, visuals, and a short pitch, meeting Ontario's Essential Employability Skills outcomes.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Week 1 — Business in Canada:** ownership forms, stakeholders, economy, ethics/CSR, Ontario regulatory basics (PCCA context, consumer protection, PIPEDA).
- **Week 2 — Functional Areas:** marketing, finance/accounting basics, operations, HR; how AI is collapsing silos between them.
- **Week 3 — The Modern Customer Engine:** SEO fundamentals, intent and keyword research, Google Ads/PPC mechanics, basic web analytics, intro to ethical web scraping for market intel.
- **Week 4 — Strategy, Entrepreneurship & AI Fluency:** lean canvas, competitive analysis, prompt patterns for business research, AI governance and bias, capstone pitch.

## AI Integration (categories — examples illustrative only)

- *Generative writing/research assistants* (such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) — drafting, summarizing, role-play coaching.
- *Search & SEO intelligence* (such as Google Search Console, Ahrefs, Semrush, SerpAPI) — keyword and SERP analysis.
- *Web scraping & data extraction* (such as Apify, Octoparse, Browse AI, or scripted scrapers) — competitive/market data; taught with robots.txt and consent guardrails.
- *Ad platforms with AI bidding* (such as Google Ads Performance Max, Meta Advantage+) — campaign concepts.
- *Spreadsheet/BI copilots* (such as Excel Copilot, Sheets Gemini, Power BI Copilot) — unit-economics modeling.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Canadian Business Teardown** (10-page report): pick a Markham/GTA SME; map ownership, value chain, customers; include AI-assisted SEO/SERP snapshot and an ethically scraped competitor pricing table with citations.
2. **Mini Go-to-Market Plan + Mock Google Ads Campaign:** ICP, keyword cluster, three ad groups, landing-page wireframe, projected CAC/LTV.
3. **AI-Augmented Pitch Deck (8 min):** lean canvas for a student-conceived venture, with an "AI usage log" appendix.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

Graded 30% project 1, 30% project 2, 25% capstone pitch, 15% participation/quizzes. **AI is required** in all projects; students submit an **AI Use Log** (tools, prompts, what was kept/changed) and cite AI-generated content. Unverified AI claims and scraped data without source attribution are penalized. Quizzes and one in-class writing diagnostic are closed-AI to confirm baseline competence.

## Pre/co-requisites

Admission to the BAM Diploma; Grade 12 English or equivalent / mature-student assessment. No prior business or tech background required.

## Instructor Depth Required

Bachelor's in business/marketing or equivalent; 5+ years professional experience spanning SEO, paid search, web analytics, and applied generative AI for marketing or operations; comfort teaching ethical scraping and basic data literacy; familiarity with Ontario PCCA program-delivery expectations.
