# 05 — Business Planning, Innovation & Entrepreneurship

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours over 4 weeks (4 weeks × 20 instructional hours/week).

## Learning Outcomes (PCCA 2005-aligned, outcomes-based)

1. Identify and validate a commercial opportunity using primary and secondary evidence, distinguishing customer pain from founder assumption.
2. Apply lean-startup and Business Model Canvas frameworks to iterate from problem hypothesis to minimum viable offer.
3. Build a 24-month cash flow projection and three-statement financial model appropriate for an Ontario micro-enterprise.
4. Produce a funder-ready business plan that meets the structural expectations of Futurpreneur, BDC, and OSBN Starter Company Plus.
5. Use AI tools ethically and transparently to augment (not replace) market research, customer synthesis, modeling, and prototyping.
6. Deliver a 7-minute investor pitch with Q&A defence.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Week 1 — Opportunity & Customer Discovery:** Ontario 2025-26 SME landscape (Toronto's 33% entrepreneur rate, micro-enterprise dominance), problem-interview protocol (Rob Fitzpatrick's "Mom Test"), Business Model Canvas v1, 15 customer interviews minimum.
- **Week 2 — MVP, Value Proposition & Business Model:** Concierge / Wizard-of-Oz / landing-page MVPs, value-proposition design, pricing experiments, unit economics, channel and revenue model selection.
- **Week 3 — Financials & Operations:** Startup cost schedule, 24-month cash flow (Futurpreneur format), break-even, sensitivity analysis, legal structure (sole prop vs. Ontario CBCA incorporation), HST, insurance, basic compliance.
- **Week 4 — Plan, Pitch & Funder Readiness:** Executive summary, BDC/Futurpreneur narrative structure, risk register, pitch-deck design, dress rehearsal, panel pitch day.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic; categories with illustrative examples)

- *Market & competitor research automation:* e.g., LLM-driven SERP scraping, StatCan/ISED data extraction, competitor feature matrices.
- *AI customer-interview synthesis:* e.g., transcript clustering, theme extraction from 15+ interviews — learners must verify quotes against source.
- *AI-assisted financial modeling:* e.g., assumption stress-testing, formula generation, scenario narration — learners own every number.
- *No-code / AI prototyping:* e.g., AI site builders, image/UX generators, chatbot mock-flows for Wizard-of-Oz tests.
- *Pitch deck assistance:* e.g., outline drafting, design suggestions, mock-investor Q&A rehearsal.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Customer Discovery Sprint** (Week 1–2): 15 logged interviews + AI-synthesized insight memo with human verification log.
2. **MVP + Validation Experiment** (Week 2–3): Live landing page or no-code prototype with ≥30 real signal data points.
3. **Capstone Business Plan & Pitch** (Week 4): Full plan + 24-month cash flow in Futurpreneur-compatible format, defensible to a BDC/Futurpreneur intake officer.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

30% Discovery Sprint · 25% MVP experiment · 30% Capstone plan + pitch · 15% participation/peer review.
Disclosure-based AI policy: every deliverable carries an "AI Use Log" naming category of assistance and human verification steps. Undisclosed AI use = academic-integrity violation per Dalwin policy and PCCA 2005 student-protection standards. Fabricated citations or financials = automatic fail of the component.

## Pre/co-requisites

Pre: Introduction to Business, Financial Accounting Fundamentals. Co: Marketing Principles.

## Instructor Depth Required

Ontario-credentialed instructor with ≥5 years founder, lender, or SBEC advisor experience; demonstrated fluency with current AI founder workflows; familiarity with Futurpreneur/BDC intake. Guest practitioner from a Markham/York Region Small Business Enterprise Centre recommended.
