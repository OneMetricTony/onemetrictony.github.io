# 02 — Business Communications

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours / 4 weeks. Foundational course, shared with the Digital Marketing diploma.

## Learning Outcomes

By course end, students will be able to:
1. Produce clear, audience-tailored business documents (emails, memos, reports, proposals, briefs) that meet Canadian professional standards and plain-language conventions.
2. Plan and execute multi-channel communications (internal, client-facing, public/social) aligned with brand voice and measurable marketing objectives.
3. Apply generative-AI tools ethically as a drafting/research aid while critically editing for accuracy, tone, bias, and Canadian/Ontario regulatory context (CASL, AODA, PIPEDA).
4. Deliver persuasive spoken communications — pitches, client meetings, stakeholder updates — with appropriate visuals.
5. Adapt messaging for SEO discoverability and paid-search ad copy constraints (character limits, keyword intent, compliance disclaimers).
6. Diagnose and resolve workplace communication breakdowns using conflict-resolution and intercultural frameworks.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Week 1 — Foundations:** Communication models, audience analysis, plain language, Canadian business etiquette, AODA-accessible writing, email/memo/report structure, grammar diagnostic.
- **Week 2 — Persuasive & Marketing Writing:** Proposals, RFP responses, sales copy, landing pages, SEO/SEM ad copy (Google/Meta character constraints), CASL-compliant outreach, AI-assisted ideation vs. human voice.
- **Week 3 — Multi-Channel & Visual Comms:** Social/blog/newsletter strategy, content calendars, data storytelling with charts, slide design, brand-voice guides, crisis comms basics.
- **Week 4 — Oral, Interpersonal & Capstone:** Pitching, meeting facilitation, negotiation, intercultural and remote-team comms, feedback/conflict, mock client presentation, capstone delivery.

## AI Integration (categories, not products)

- Large-language-model writing assistants (such as ChatGPT, Copilot, Gemini).
- AI grammar/style editors (e.g., Grammarly-class).
- AI research/summarization tools (e.g., Perplexity-class).
- AI image/slide generators (e.g., Canva-AI, Gamma-class).
- AI SEO/ad-copy assistants (e.g., Surfer, Semrush AI — illustrative only).

Students learn prompt engineering, hallucination-checking, and Canadian-context fact-verification.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **AI-assisted RFP response** — student team responds to a mock municipal RFP; AI used for outline/first draft, human-edited final, with a 1-page reflection on what AI got wrong.
2. **Multi-channel communications plan** — for a fictional Markham SMB: brand-voice guide, 4-week content calendar, 3 Google Ads variants, 1 landing page, 1 CASL-compliant email sequence.
3. **Live client pitch** — 10-minute presentation + slide deck + executive summary, delivered to a panel.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

- Participation/in-class writing 15% | RFP project 20% | Multi-channel plan 25% | Pitch + deck 20% | Final written exam (handwritten, no AI) 20%.
- **AI required** on RFP and multi-channel projects (must disclose tool, prompts used, and edits made — citation in APA "personal communication / generative AI" format).
- **AI prohibited** on the final exam, in-class writing diagnostics, and oral pitch delivery.
- Undisclosed AI use = academic-misconduct review per Dalwin policy and Career Colleges Act s.42 record-keeping.

## Pre/co-requisites

None (foundational); co-requisite recommended: *Introduction to Business* or *Digital Marketing Fundamentals*.

## Instructor Depth Required

Minimum bachelor's degree in Communications, Marketing, English, or Business + 3 years industry experience in B2B/B2C marketing communications, agency, or corporate comms; demonstrated working fluency with generative-AI tooling and Canadian compliance (CASL, AODA). PCC instructor approval per O. Reg. 415/06.
