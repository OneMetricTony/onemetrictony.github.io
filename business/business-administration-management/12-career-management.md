# 12 — Career Management

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours / 4 weeks (final term, capstone-adjacent). Shared with the Digital Marketing diploma.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion, students will:
1. Conduct a structured self-assessment (skills, values, transferable competencies) and map results to Ontario NOC codes and target sectors via Ontario Job Bank.
2. Produce ATS-aware, role-tailored resumes, cover letters, and a LinkedIn profile that pass keyword/parsing checks while remaining truthful and disclosed.
3. Execute a multi-channel Ontario job search (Job Bank, WorkInCulture/WorkInON, sector councils, employer ATS portals) including newcomer pathways (credential recognition via WES/ICAS, bridging programs, Ontario Bridge Training).
4. Demonstrate interview, salary-negotiation, and onboarding competence aligned to Canadian workplace norms, including disclosure obligations under Ontario's Working for Workers Four Act (Bill 149).
5. Build a 12-month professional development plan with measurable goals, networks, and credential renewal triggers.
6. Maintain PARIS-ready employment evidence (offer letters, NOC, hours, wage, start date) supporting Dalwin's PCCA 2005 KPI reporting.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **W1:** Self-assessment + labour-market scan (Ontario Job Bank, ESDC outlooks); career portfolio scaffolding; newcomer credential recognition.
- **W2:** Resume/cover letter/LinkedIn build; ATS literacy (parsing, keyword density, formatting traps); personal branding.
- **W3:** Active job search (WorkInCulture, WorkInON, Indeed Canada, sector portals); networking + informational interviews; mock interviews (behavioural, STAR, technical screen).
- **W4:** Negotiation (range research, counter-offer scripts); onboarding 30/60/90 plan; ongoing PD, micro-credentials, union/association membership; PARIS exit documentation.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic, categories with examples)

- *Resume optimization vs ATS:* keyword-gap analyzers (e.g., Jobscan-style match scoring) — students learn the limits and bias risks.
- *Mock-interview agents:* conversational AI for behavioural/technical drills with rubric feedback.
- *AI-tailored cover letters:* prompt-engineered drafts students must edit and fact-check.
- *Networking outreach:* AI-assisted personalized InMail/email drafting from job posting + profile inputs.
- *LinkedIn content:* AI-suggested headlines, About sections, and weekly thought-leadership posts.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Job-Ready Portfolio** (BAM: pitch deck, ops case, KPI dashboard) — peer-juried.
2. **Mock Interview Reel** — three recorded sessions (behavioural, case/technical, panel) with self-critique and AI-feedback log.
3. **90-Day Onboarding & 12-Month PD Plan** tied to a real target offer or simulated role.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

Portfolio 30% · mock-interview reel 20% · applied job-search log (10 documented applications + outreach) 25% · negotiation/onboarding plan 15% · professionalism 10%.
Every deliverable requires an **AI-Use Disclosure Statement** (tool, purpose, prompts, human edits) modelling Bill 149 disclosure norms. Undisclosed AI use = academic-integrity violation. Fabricated experience = automatic fail.

## Pre/co-requisites

Completion of Term 1-2 communications and program-core courses; co-requisite with capstone/practicum.

## Instructor Depth Required

Minimum 5 years Ontario hiring/recruiting or career-coaching experience (CDP/CCDP credential preferred), current familiarity with ATS platforms, Ontario employment law (ESA, Bill 149), newcomer settlement pathways, and Dalwin PARIS reporting workflow.
