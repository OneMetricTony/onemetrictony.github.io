# 06 — Business Capstone Project 1

**Hours / weeks:** 120 hours over 6 weeks (≈20 h/week classroom + supervised studio), positioned in weeks 37–42 of the 48-week BAM diploma; counts toward the PCCA-required practical/applied component.

## Learning Outcomes

On completion, learners will be able to:
1. Scope a real client business problem into a written Statement of Work with measurable success criteria.
2. Integrate concepts from prior BAM coursework (Marketing, Accounting/Finance, Operations, HR, Business Analytics) into a coherent diagnostic.
3. Conduct primary and secondary discovery research (stakeholder interviews, market scans, internal data review) using a documented methodology.
4. Build a quantitative analysis (financial model, KPI dashboard, or process map) supporting at least three prioritized recommendations.
5. Communicate findings in a client-grade written report and a boardroom-style executive presentation.
6. Demonstrate professional conduct, ethical AI use, and reflective practice across the engagement.

## Project Structure (phase-by-phase, milestone-gated)

- **Phase 1 — Scoping (Wk 1):** Client matching, kickoff meeting, signed SOW. *Milestone:* SOW + project charter approved by instructor and client sponsor.
- **Phase 2 — Discovery (Wk 2):** Stakeholder interviews, document review, industry benchmarking. *Milestone:* Discovery brief + interview log.
- **Phase 3 — Analysis (Wk 3–4):** Root-cause diagnostic, SWOT/Porter, financial or operational modeling. *Milestone:* Mid-project client checkpoint deck.
- **Phase 4 — Recommendations (Wk 5):** Prioritized options, implementation roadmap, risk register, ROI estimate. *Milestone:* Draft report reviewed in peer studio.
- **Phase 5 — Presentation & Handover (Wk 6):** Final client presentation, Q&A, deliverable handover, reflection. *Milestone:* Signed client acceptance form.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic; categories with examples)

- *Research:* generative search assistants to summarize industry reports and competitor websites.
- *Synthesis:* clustering interview notes and document excerpts into themes via an LLM workspace.
- *Modeling:* AI copilots inside a spreadsheet to draft formulas, sensitivity tables, or forecast scaffolds (student must verify math).
- *Writing:* AI editing assistants for tone, structure, and plain-language rewriting of the client report.
- *Slide design:* AI presentation generators to draft layouts and visuals from outline text; student finalizes data integrity and branding.

## Deliverables

1. Client-style consulting report (25–35 pp incl. appendices).
2. Executive presentation (15-min deck + 10-min Q&A) delivered to client sponsor and faculty panel.
3. Reflection journal (weekly entries + final 1,500-word synthesis on learning, teamwork, and AI use).

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

AI use is **required and disclosed**. Each deliverable includes an AI-Use Appendix listing tools, prompts, and how outputs were verified. Undisclosed use = academic misconduct.

Rubric: Client Report 35% / Executive Presentation 25% / Analytical Rigor & Data Integrity 15% / Professionalism & Client Management 10% / Reflection Journal & AI Disclosure 15%.

## Pre/co-requisites

Successful completion of BAM core courses in Marketing, Financial Accounting, Business Analytics, Organizational Behaviour, and Business Communications. Co-requisite: Professional Practice Seminar.

## Instructor Depth Required

PCCA-compliant — minimum 48 months of consulting, management, or related vocational experience within the past 10 years; demonstrated client-facing project leadership; familiarity with generative-AI tools and disclosure norms.
