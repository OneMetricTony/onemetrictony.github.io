# 07 — Computer Applications

**Hours / weeks:** 80h / 4 weeks (16h/wk; ~12h instructor-led + 4h lab). Sits in Term 1 as a foundational course; shared with the Digital Marketing diploma.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:
1. Operate a modern cloud-based office suite (such as Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, or equivalent) to produce business-grade documents, spreadsheets, presentations, and shared workspaces.
2. Apply intermediate spreadsheet techniques (structured tables, lookup/logic functions, pivot tables, basic charts and dashboards) to analyze business data.
3. Use generative-AI assistants embedded in chat, office, and browser tools to draft, summarize, analyze, and verify business content while disclosing AI use appropriately.
4. Build a simple no-code/low-code automation that connects two or more business apps (such as Power Automate, Apps Script, Zapier, or equivalent).
5. Demonstrate baseline data literacy and lawful information-gathering, including the difference between APIs, exported data, and web scraping, with awareness of copyright and PIPEDA/Ontario privacy rules.
6. Apply core cybersecurity hygiene (MFA, password managers, phishing/deepfake recognition, safe handling of confidential data in AI tools).

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Wk 1 — Office Suite & Cloud Collaboration:** file management, styles, mail merge, shared drives, version history, accessibility checks; Teams/Meet/Chat etiquette.
- **Wk 2 — Data Analysis Basics:** tables, IF/IFS, XLOOKUP, SUMIFS, pivot tables, charts, simple dashboards; intro to forms/surveys feeding sheets.
- **Wk 3 — Automation & AI Productivity:** AI in office suites (draft/summarize/translate, slide generation, Excel formula explain, Sheets "Help me organize"); LLM chat for research and writing; document-Q&A over PDFs; browser/automation copilots; building one Power Automate / Apps Script flow.
- **Wk 4 — Data Literacy, Scraping Concepts & Cyber Hygiene:** evaluating sources, citation, hallucination checks; APIs vs CSV exports vs scraping (legal/ethical limits, robots.txt, ToS); passwords/MFA, phishing & deepfake voice/email, safe prompts (no PII/client data), Canadian privacy basics.

## AI Integration (categories with examples)

- *LLM chat:* such as ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or Copilot Chat.
- *AI in office suites:* such as Microsoft 365 Copilot or Gemini for Workspace.
- *Spreadsheet copilots:* such as Excel Copilot or Sheets Gemini.
- *Document-Q&A:* such as NotebookLM or Copilot "chat with this file".
- *Browser/automation copilots:* such as Edge/Chrome AI sidebars and Power Automate / Apps Script AI-assisted flow building.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **AI-assisted financial model:** build a 12-month small-business cash-flow workbook using AI to generate formulas, then manually verify and stress-test.
2. **Automated report pipeline:** a Form/Sheet/Doc flow that collects data, formats a weekly summary, and emails stakeholders (BAM stream emphasizes ops reporting).
3. **AI literacy mini-audit:** compare two LLM outputs on the same business prompt; identify factual errors, bias, and missing citations.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

30% in-class skills checks · 40% projects · 20% quizzes · 10% professionalism.
AI use is *required and disclosed*: every submission includes an "AI Use Statement" (tools, prompts, what was AI-generated vs. student-verified). Fabricated citations and undisclosed AI use are academic misconduct.

## Pre/co-requisites

None; recommended basic keyboarding (~25 wpm). Co-requisite: Business Communications.

## Instructor Depth Required

Diploma/degree in business, IT, or education *plus* 3+ years of office-environment experience; demonstrated working use of M365 Copilot or Gemini for Workspace; comfort teaching one low-code automation platform. Annual PD on generative-AI tools required for PCCA 2005 program-quality compliance.
