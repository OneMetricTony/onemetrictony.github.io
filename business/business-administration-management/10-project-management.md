# 10 — Project Management

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours / 4 weeks (intensive block); 5 instructional hours/day × 4 days/week + 4h guided lab. Shared core with the Digital Marketing diploma.

## Learning Outcomes

By completion, learners will be able to:
1. Apply the twelve PMBOK 7th edition principles and eight performance domains to plan and govern a small-to-mid project end-to-end.
2. Produce core artifacts: project charter, WBS, schedule, budget estimate, RAID log, communications plan, and closure report.
3. Compare and select predictive, Agile/Scrum, Kanban, and hybrid delivery approaches against project context and stakeholder needs.
4. Facilitate Scrum events (sprint planning, daily standup, review, retrospective) and run a Kanban board with WIP limits.
5. Integrate AI assistants ethically into planning, risk, status, and meeting workflows while preserving human accountability.
6. Communicate project status, escalations, and value delivery to technical and non-technical stakeholders.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **W1:** Project lifecycles & PMBOK 7 principles; initiation, business case, stakeholder identification, charter drafting.
- **W2:** Scope & WBS; estimation (analogous, parametric, three-point); scheduling (network diagrams, critical path); cost baselining; quantitative & qualitative risk; stakeholder engagement & communications plan.
- **W3:** Adaptive delivery: Agile Manifesto, Scrum roles/events/artifacts, Kanban flow, hybrid tailoring; team dynamics and servant leadership.
- **W4:** Monitoring/control, EVM basics, status reporting & dashboards, change control, closure, lessons learned; capstone presentations.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic; examples illustrative only)

- AI-assisted planning/estimation: generate draft WBS and three-point estimates from a charter; learner critiques and adjusts.
- Status report generation: synthesize task/board/timesheet exports into a stakeholder-ready weekly status.
- Risk surface scanning: prompt an LLM to enumerate category-based risks (technical, supply, regulatory) for review against PMBOK risk domain.
- Meeting intelligence/summaries: AI transcription producing action items and decisions from a recorded standup or sponsor meeting.
- RAID log automation: AI extracts Risks/Assumptions/Issues/Dependencies from meeting notes and emails; learner validates and assigns owners.
- Autonomous PM agents (awareness only): discussion of 2026 agentic PM trends and Gartner's caution that >40% of agentic AI projects may be cancelled by 2027.

## Hands-on Projects

1. End-to-end charter + plan package for a simulated Markham SMB client (BAM: retail expansion; DM: website relaunch) — charter, WBS, schedule, budget, RAID, comms plan.
2. Two-sprint Scrum simulation in cross-functional teams with AI-generated backlog drafts, AI meeting summaries, and a retrospective on AI accuracy.
3. Status & closure deliverable: AI-drafted weekly status reports (3 cycles) plus a final closure report with lessons learned.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

Charter/plan 25% · Scrum simulation 20% · status report portfolio 15% · risk + estimation quiz 10% · capstone presentation 20% · professionalism/participation 10%.
AI permitted for drafting, summarization, and ideation; all AI-assisted artifacts require a disclosure block (tool category, prompt summary, learner edits). Prohibited for closed-book quizzes and reflective journals. Learner remains accountable for accuracy; uncritical paste-through is treated as an academic integrity issue per Ontario PCCA 2005 institutional policy.

## Pre/co-requisites

Pre: Business Communications, Computer Fundamentals (spreadsheets). Co: Professional Practice & Ethics.

## Instructor Depth Required

Minimum 5 years applied PM experience; PMP, PMI-ACP, or PRINCE2 Practitioner preferred; documented exposure to Scrum/Kanban delivery and to GenAI-assisted PM workflows.
