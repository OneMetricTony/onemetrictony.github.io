# Responsible AI & Digital Ethics (Cross-Cutting Module)

**Total hours:** ~20 dedicated + threaded through every course (~2h per course) = ~68h total across each 960h diploma

## Module Learning Outcomes
1. Identify and apply Canadian and international AI/privacy obligations (PIPEDA, Quebec Law 25, Ontario Bill 149, Ontario Bill 194, OECD AI Principles, NIST AI RMF 1.0) to workplace decisions.
2. Evaluate AI systems for bias, fairness, transparency, and explainability using a structured risk-assessment method (Govern–Map–Measure–Manage).
3. Draft disclosure, consent, and acceptable-use language that satisfies Ontario hiring-disclosure rules and employer policy standards.
4. Distinguish legitimate, ethically sourced AI use from prohibited use (deepfakes of real persons, scraped copyrighted material, undisclosed automated decisions).
5. Reflect on professional codes of conduct (CPA, CIM, CMA, IAPP, HRPA) and articulate personal AI-use boundaries in writing.
6. Demonstrate human-in-the-loop verification practices that uphold Ontario PCCA 2005 academic-integrity expectations.

## Dedicated Content Topics (~20h, six lessons)
1. **Canadian Regulatory Landscape (4h):** AIDA died with Bill C-27 prorogation Jan 2025; replacement signals; Ontario Bill 194; PIPEDA; Quebec Law 25 ADM disclosure; Ontario Bill 149 effective 1 Jan 2026.
2. **Frameworks (3h):** NIST AI RMF 1.0, OECD AI Principles, Treasury Board FASTER.
3. **Bias, Fairness, Transparency (3h):** disparate impact, explainability, model cards.
4. **IP, Copyright, Scraping Ethics (3h):** training-data provenance, Canadian Copyright Act fair-dealing limits.
5. **Deepfakes, Voice, Likeness, Environmental Impact, Data Minimization (4h).**
6. **AI Safety Basics & Professional Codes of Conduct (3h):** HRPA, CIM, IAPP-Canada.

## AI Integration (ironic but necessary)

Instructors model disclosed AI use. Students use a tool-agnostic chatbot to generate a draft policy then critique its hallucinations; an image generator produces a flawed marketing asset students must identify as deepfake; lecture transcripts are AI-summarized with a visible disclosure footer; every AI artifact carries a provenance log students fill in.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **AI Use Audit** of a hypothetical Markham SME employer: map every AI touchpoint against NIST Map, flag Bill 149 disclosure gaps, recommend remediations (week 20).
2. **Responsible-AI Policy Draft** for a fictional organization: includes acceptable-use, Law 25 cross-border clauses, incident reporting, employee training plan (week 40).

## Assessment

- Audit project 30%
- Policy draft 30%
- Weekly threaded reflections 20%
- Final scenario exam covering Bill 149 / PIPEDA / Law 25 application 20%
- Pass mark 60% per PCCA 2005 quality-assurance standards
- Rubric aligned to learning outcomes
- Integrity violations adjudicated under the college's Student Code consistent with Ontario PCCA 2005 s.42 grievance procedures

## Connection to Ontario Regulatory Context

Module is mapped to PCCA 2005 program-approval requirements (clear outcomes, hours, assessment). Bill 149 is operationalised in the HR thread and audit project (mandatory AI disclosure in postings, 25+ employee threshold). PIPEDA frames all personal-information handling. Quebec Law 25 is applied any time student employers operate in QC (ADM disclosure on request, cross-border transfer assessments). AIDA's lapsed status is taught as a live policy-watch item, not as enforceable law, with students tracking replacement legislation.
