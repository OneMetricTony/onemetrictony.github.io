# 02 — Digital Psychology

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours over 4 weeks (~20 h/week).

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, learners will be able to:
1. Explain core attention, perception, memory, and dual-process (System 1/2) models as they apply to digital interfaces.
2. Identify cognitive biases and behavioural-economics principles (anchoring, loss aversion, social proof, default effects) in real consumer journeys.
3. Apply persuasion frameworks (Cialdini's 7 principles, Fogg Behaviour Model B=MAP, COM-B, EAST) to ethical funnel and UX design.
4. Distinguish ethical persuasion from deceptive design ("dark patterns") using OECD, Canadian Competition Bureau, and OPC criteria.
5. Plan and interpret behavioural experiments (A/B, multivariate, holdouts) and translate findings into compliant recommendations.
6. Draft an AI-personalization plan that respects PIPEDA, Ontario consumer-protection law, and PCCA 2005 professional-conduct expectations.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Wk 1:** Attention/perception, working memory, Hick's law; cognitive biases; behavioural economics foundations (Kahneman, Thaler).
- **Wk 2:** Persuasion frameworks (Cialdini, Fogg, EAST, Hook Model); UX psychology — mental models, friction, micro-copy, emotion.
- **Wk 3:** Personalization and segmentation psychology; recommender logic; nudges vs. sludge; dark-patterns taxonomy; Competition Bureau and OPC guidance on deceptive design; ethics charter drafting.
- **Wk 4:** Behavioural measurement — hypothesis design, power, novelty/primacy effects, Bayesian vs. frequentist A/B reporting; capstone presentations.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic, examples only)

- *AI personalization engines* — dynamic content, next-best-action (e.g., generative product recommendations).
- *AI-driven experimentation* — auto-allocation bandits, synthetic-control test design.
- *Sentiment and intent analysis* — LLM classification of reviews, session transcripts, support chats.
- *Conversational persuasion* — chatbot scripting, voice-assistant tone, guardrails against manipulative anthropomorphism.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Behavioural audit** of a public e-commerce or SaaS site: map biases, flag suspected dark patterns against Competition Bureau / OPC criteria, propose ethical fixes.
2. **Ethical AI personalization plan** for a fictional Canadian brand: segmentation logic, consent flow, opt-outs, fairness checks, PIPEDA-aligned data minimization.
3. **A/B test report** (synthetic dataset): hypothesis, metric tree, sample size, results write-up with ethical-risk section.

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

30% behavioural audit · 30% personalization plan · 25% A/B report · 15% participation/quizzes.
Generative AI permitted for ideation, drafting, and code scaffolding when disclosed in an AI-Use Log (tool, prompt summary, edits). Undisclosed AI output, fabricated citations, or AI-generated user data without disclosure constitute academic misconduct. All client/persona data must be synthetic; no scraping of real personal data.

## Pre/co-requisites

Pre-req: Foundations of Digital Marketing, Consumer Research Methods. Co-req: UX & Content Design or Analytics Fundamentals.

## Instructor Depth Required

Minimum bachelor's in psychology, behavioural science, marketing, HCI, or related field plus 3+ years applied CRO/UX/behavioural-design practice; demonstrated familiarity with PIPEDA, Competition Act misleading-practices provisions, and Ontario Consumer Protection Act.
