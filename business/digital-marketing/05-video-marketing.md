# 05 — Video Marketing

**Hours / weeks:** 80 hours / 4 weeks (20h/week).

## Learning Outcomes

1. Develop platform-specific video marketing strategies aligned to YouTube, TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts audience behaviours and algorithmic signals.
2. Plan and produce both long-form (8–15 min) and short-form (15–60 sec) marketing video using smartphone-grade gear, three-point lighting and clean audio capture.
3. Apply generative AI across the production pipeline (ideation, scripting, footage generation, editing, captioning, dubbing, thumbnails) while documenting provenance.
4. Build, target and optimize YouTube Ads campaigns (Skippable In-Stream, Bumper, In-Feed, Shorts Ads) using cost-per-view and view-through conversion metrics.
5. Interpret video analytics (retention curves, AVD, CTR, completion rate, saves) to iterate creative.
6. Comply with Canadian regulatory and ethical norms: CRTC Online Streaming Act discoverability, Competition Bureau influencer disclosure, deepfake transparency and personality/likeness rights.

## Weekly Topic Breakdown

- **Wk 1** Video marketing strategy, audience/funnel mapping, platform comparison (YouTube vs TikTok vs Reels vs Shorts), long vs short-form formats; pre-production: briefs, scripts, storyboards, shot lists.
- **Wk 2** Production fundamentals: smartphone cinematography, three-point lighting, lavalier/shotgun audio, B-roll; AI-augmented production (text-to-video, image-to-video, AI b-roll, voice cloning).
- **Wk 3** Editing (cut, pacing, j/l cuts, hook in 3s), motion graphics, captioning/translation, thumbnail design; distribution (publishing cadence, hashtags, descriptions, end-screens, cross-posting Reels/Shorts/TikTok).
- **Wk 4** YouTube Ads (campaign types, audience signals, bidding, frequency caps), measurement (YT Studio, TikTok Analytics, Meta Insights), A/B thumbnail tests, iteration sprint, ethics & Canadian compliance.

## AI Integration (tool-agnostic categories with examples)

- *Text-to-video / image-to-video:* e.g., Veo 3, Runway Gen-4, Kling, Pika, Seedance.
- *AI avatars & dubbing:* e.g., HeyGen, Synthesia, ElevenLabs voice; multilingual lip-sync.
- *AI editing & rough cuts:* e.g., Descript, Adobe Premiere Generative Extend, CapCut AI, Opus Clip (long-to-short repurposing).
- *Captioning & translation:* e.g., auto-SRT generation, on-screen kinetic captions.
- *Thumbnail / cover generation:* e.g., Midjourney + face-swap workflows, Canva AI.
- *Performance prediction & hook testing:* e.g., VidIQ AI, TubeBuddy, TikTok Symphony, retention-curve forecasting.

## Hands-on Projects

1. **Brand short-form series** (5 vertical videos, 1 per platform variant) with hook test and caption translation.
2. **AI-generated explainer** (60-90 sec) combining text-to-video clips, AI avatar host, and human voice-over; provenance log required.
3. **YouTube channel launch plan + first Ad campaign** (channel kit, 2 long-form videos, 1 ad creative set, $50 simulated budget, optimization memo).

## Assessment & AI-Use Policy

40% projects · 25% campaign portfolio · 20% analytics report · 15% participation/quizzes.

Students must disclose AI-generated footage, voices and avatars in on-screen text or description (deepfake disclosure). Voice and likeness require signed release; no AI cloning of real persons without written consent. Misleading synthetic endorsements prohibited under Competition Act s.74.01 and Bill C-59 amendments. CRTC discoverability and Cancon labelling discussed for hosted distribution.

## Pre/co-requisites

Content Marketing & Copywriting; Social Media Marketing (co-req acceptable).

## Instructor Depth Required

5+ yrs commercial video/social media production; demonstrated YouTube Ads (Google Ads Video certification) and live AI-pipeline experience; familiarity with PIPEDA, Competition Bureau influencer guidance and CRTC 2026-96.
