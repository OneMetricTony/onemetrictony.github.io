# Assessment Framework & Generative AI Use Policy

**Dalwin College — Markham, ON** | Applies to: Business Administration & Management (BAM, 960h/48wk) and Digital Marketing (DM, 960h/48wk) | Registered under the *Ontario Career Colleges Act, 2005* (OCCA, formerly PCCA 2005) and O. Reg. 415/06.

## Part 1 — Assessment Framework

### 1.1 Outcomes-Based Assessment Principles
Every graded task must map to a published Vocational Learning Outcome (VLO) approved by the Ministry of Colleges and Universities (MCU) at program registration. Faculty document outcome-to-assessment alignment in the Course Information Sheet (CIS). Assessment evidence must be *valid* (measures the outcome), *reliable* (consistent across cohorts), *authentic* (reflects workplace tasks in business operations or digital campaign delivery), and *sufficient* (multiple evidence points per VLO).

### 1.2 Weighting Standards
- **Foundations / theory courses:** Formative (ungraded checks) ~20%; Quizzes/Tests 30%; Applied assignments 40%; Participation/professionalism 10%.
- **Applied / lab courses (e.g., BAM Financial Accounting, DM Paid Media Lab):** Formative 10%; Skills demonstrations 50%; Project deliverables 30%; Reflection 10%.
- **Capstone (final 8 weeks of each diploma):** Industry-scoped project 60%; Pitch/defense 20%; Portfolio 15%; Professionalism 5%.
- No single assessment may exceed 40% of the final grade (except capstone defense). Pass = 60% per course; capstone pass = 70%.

### 1.3 Required Assessment Types
Each course must include at least:
(a) two formative low-stakes checks;
(b) one applied/authentic task (case, simulation, client brief);
(c) one summative integrated assessment.
Each diploma culminates in a *capstone* requiring oral defense before a faculty-plus-industry panel.

### 1.4 Rubric Scaffolding
All summative tasks use a four-level analytic rubric (Beginning / Developing / Proficient / Exemplary) with criteria written as observable behaviours tied to the VLO. Rubrics are released to students *before* the task opens. Sample anchors are archived for moderation.

### 1.5 Attendance & Completion (OCCA / O. Reg. 415/06)
Minimum 80% attendance is required to remain in good standing; below 70% triggers a documented academic intervention. Per O. Reg. 415/06 s. 25, the contract may be cancelled if a student does not attend within the first 14 days. Attendance is recorded daily and retained in the student file alongside the academic record, grades per course/practicum, and grading system description (s. 45). Students completing fewer than 70% of scheduled hours are ineligible for the diploma.

## Part 2 — Generative AI Use Policy

### 2.1 Three-Tier Classification
Every assessment must carry one tag on its instructions:
- 🔴 **AI-Prohibited (Red):** No generative AI permitted (e.g., invigilated tests, accounting calculations, foundational writing diagnostics). Use = academic misconduct.
- 🟠 **AI-Permitted with Disclosure (Amber):** Allowed for brainstorming, outlining, code/copy drafting, image mockups — *only* with the disclosure template completed.
- 🟢 **AI-Required (Green):** Students must use a generative tool (e.g., DM "AI-assisted campaign brief," BAM "AI-augmented market scan") and critique its output.

### 2.2 Mandatory AI Use Disclosure Template
Paste into every Amber/Green submission:

> *Tool family used (e.g., general-purpose chat LLM): __*
> *Purpose (ideation / drafting / editing / data analysis / image): __*
> *Sections affected: __*
> *Representative prompts: __*
> *Human verification performed: __*
> *Confidence in outputs: __*

### 2.3 Citation Convention
Generative AI outputs are not retrievable sources. Following APA 7 guidance adopted across Ontario colleges, cite as a *personal communication* in-text — e.g., "(Generative AI tool, personal communication, [date])" — with a methods-note describing prompts. Quoted outputs require quotation marks.

### 2.4 Academic Integrity Escalation
- **1st offence:** Educational conference with instructor + zero on task + file notation.
- **2nd offence:** Failure of course + mandatory Academic Integrity module + Registrar hold.
- **3rd offence:** Suspension or expulsion per Dalwin Student Conduct Policy; OCCA-compliant withdrawal procedures apply.

### 2.5 Faculty Obligations
Instructors must:
(a) tag every assessment Red/Amber/Green;
(b) redesign assessments annually to remain meaningful when AI is available (more in-class, oral, iterative, and process-evidenced work);
(c) avoid sole reliance on AI-detection tools.

### 2.6 Data Privacy (PIPEDA)
Students must not input personal information of classmates, clients, placement hosts, or themselves (SIN, health, financial records) into public cloud AI tools. Practicum/client data is confidential by default. Dalwin will not require students to create accounts on tools that transmit identifiable data outside Canada without an opt-out alternative.

## Part 3 — PARIS / KPI Reporting Connection

Course-level assessment data feeds Dalwin's annual MCU **PARIS** (Private Career Colleges Annual Report Information System) submission supporting the five PCC KPIs: graduation rate, graduate employment rate, employment-in-field rate, graduate satisfaction, and OSAP default rate. Capstone outcomes and employer-panel feedback are stored as evidence for the *employment-in-field* indicator. Per O. Reg. 415/06 s. 45–46, student academic records, attendance registers, contracts, and fee receipts are retained **25 years** (transcripts permanently); assessment artefacts and rubrics are retained **7 years** for audit and KPI verification.

## Part 4 — Implementation Checklist

**Program Coordinators:** Confirm VLO-to-assessment map; verify Red/Amber/Green tagging on every CIS; audit rubric library each semester; reconcile attendance registers monthly; submit PARIS data package.

**Instructors:** Publish rubrics with task release; collect AI Disclosure Statements; log integrity incidents within 5 business days; retain graded samples (high/mid/low) per course.

**Students:** Track attendance; complete the AI Disclosure template on every Amber/Green submission; cite AI outputs in APA personal-communication form; protect personal and client data; review the Academic Integrity Procedure each term.
