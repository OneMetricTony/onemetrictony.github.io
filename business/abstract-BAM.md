## What this program is

Dalwin's Business Administration & Management diploma is a **practitioner-led, AI-integrated** 960-hour program for Ontario adult learners — newcomers, career-changers, and recent grads. It begins with an **80-hour AI Foundations bootcamp** that teaches the timeless principles of artificial intelligence — without depending on any specific vendor — so graduates remain employable as tools change every six months. Each of the twelve subsequent courses follows a **[MAIN IDEA] → [MAIN IDEA + AI]** structure: students first learn the business function the way a 1990s textbook taught it, then learn how AI is reshaping that function today, then build with AI under explicit Canadian regulatory guardrails.

## Why it exists now

The World Economic Forum's *Future of Jobs 2025* lists AI/Big Data as the **#1 fastest-growing skill** [WEF]. BCG documents that **5 hours of AI training pushes regular-use rates from 67% to 79%** — Dalwin's 80-hour bootcamp clears that threshold many times over [BCG]. McKinsey reports **46% of organizations cite skill gaps as the #1 blocker to AI scaling** [McKinsey]. Meanwhile, Ontario Bill 149 (effective January 1, 2026) mandates AI disclosure on hiring postings — yet **most Ontario private career colleges teach zero AI** (CDI, Anderson, Westervelt) and public colleges teach implicit AI buried inside legacy digital-marketing modules. Dalwin closes the gap with explicit AI titles, principles-first pedagogy, and Canadian-compliance literacy from day one.

## Hours allocation (research-backed)

Hours follow the Ontario college norm of **42-hour standard modules, 56-hour intensive labs, and 84-120-hour applied capstones** validated across Conestoga, Sheridan, Algonquin, Mohawk, George Brown, and Centennial course outlines. The program is **not uniform**: a 4-hour-per-week Saturday computer-applications lab is denser than a 3-hour-per-week Monday morning lecture on organizational behaviour. Hours communicate workload at a glance.

| Component | Hours | Format |
|---|---|---|
| AI Foundations Bootcamp | 56 | 4 weeks, principles-only, no vendor lock |
| 12 core BAM courses (all AI-titled) | 644 | Mix of 28h / 42h / 56h / 84h based on outcome depth |
| Industry internship | 240 | Real Markham/GTA employer placement, AI-augmented deliverables |
| Portfolio Capstone artifact | 20 | Employer-graded portfolio piece |
| **TOTAL** | **960** | 48 weeks |

## Graduate outcomes

Graduates target Ontario NOC 11201 Business Operations Analyst (median $44.94/hr) and NOC 11202 Marketing/PR Professional (median $37-$40/hr) [Ontario Job Bank]. Dalwin's curriculum explicitly trains the AI-skill premium documented at +12-18% in wages [ESDC]. By 2027, **75% of hiring processes will include AI-proficiency testing** [Gartner] — Dalwin graduates arrive pre-fluent.

## What makes this diploma different

1. **Principles first, tools last.** Every concept is taught vendor-neutral. Tools are illustrated, never required.
2. **Ontario compliance is built in.** PIPEDA, CASL, Quebec Law 25, Bill 149, OECD, UNESCO, and NIST AI RMF are threaded through every course — not bolted on as a single ethics elective.
3. **AI in every title.** No course in Dalwin's BAM lacks the word AI. This signals currency to learners, employers, and Service Canada Second Career assessors.
4. **Process-based assessment.** Graded on prompt logs, draft iterations, and verification work — not final outputs an LLM could have produced.
5. **Andragogy-aware delivery.** Three schedule tracks. Wraparound supports modelled on LINC. Cohort accountability partners to combat the month-3 motivation cliff.
