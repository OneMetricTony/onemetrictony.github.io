Momentum · collisions · friction · oscillations
Question

In outer space two rocks collide and stick together:

Rock 1: \(m_1=23\ \mathrm{kg},\ \vec v_1=\langle 11,-23,0\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\)

Rock 2: \(m_2=43\ \mathrm{kg},\ \vec v_2=\langle 28,6,0\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\)

Find the velocity of the stuck-together rocks.

Worked solution

Conserve momentum (perfectly inelastic)

\[\vec p_i=m_1\vec v_1+m_2\vec v_2=23\langle 11,-23,0\rangle+43\langle 28,6,0\rangle=\langle 1457,-271,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\]

Divide by the combined mass

\[M=m_1+m_2=66\ \mathrm{kg}\]

\[\boxed{\;\vec v_f=\dfrac{\langle 1457,-271,0\rangle}{66}=\langle 22.08,\,-4.11,\,0\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\;}\]
Question

\(A\) strikes \(B\). Just before the collision:

\(m_A=11\ \mathrm{kg},\ \vec p_{A,i}=\langle 23,-8,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\)

\(m_B=14\ \mathrm{kg},\ \vec p_{B,i}=\langle 7,12,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\)

(a) total initial momentum, (b) external impulse, (c) total final momentum, (d) \(\vec p_{B,f}\) if \(\vec p_{A,f}=\langle 21,3,0\rangle\).

Worked solution

(a) Total initial momentum

\[\vec p_{sys,i}=\vec p_{A,i}+\vec p_{B,i}=\langle 23,-8,0\rangle+\langle 7,12,0\rangle=\langle 30,4,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\]

(b) External impulse

The \(A\!\leftrightarrow\!B\) forces are internal; external forces are negligible over the brief collision:

\[\vec F_{net}\,\Delta t\approx\langle 0,0,0\rangle\ \mathrm{N\cdot s}\]

(c) Total final momentum

\[\Delta\vec p_{sys}=\vec F_{net}\,\Delta t\approx 0\ \Rightarrow\ \vec p_{sys,f}=\langle 30,4,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\]

(d) Final momentum of B

\[\boxed{\;\vec p_{B,f}=\vec p_{sys,f}-\vec p_{A,f}=\langle 30,4,0\rangle-\langle 21,3,0\rangle=\langle 9,1,0\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\;}\]
Question

Two identical balls, \(m=7.5\ \mathrm{kg}\). Ball \(A\) at \(\vec v=\langle 6,0,0\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\) strikes \(B\) (at rest); then \(A\) stops and \(B\) moves at \(\langle 6,0,0\rangle\). Find \(\Delta\vec p\) of the system and the surroundings for system = A only, B only, both.

Worked solution

\[\vec p_{A,i}=m\vec v=7.5\langle 6,0,0\rangle=\langle 45,0,0\rangle,\qquad \vec p_{B,f}=\langle 45,0,0\rangle\]

System + surroundings = the universe (total momentum conserved), so \(\Delta\vec p_{surr}=-\Delta\vec p_{sys}\).

System = ball A

\[\Delta\vec p_{sys}=0-\langle 45,0,0\rangle=\langle -45,0,0\rangle,\qquad \Delta\vec p_{surr}=\langle 45,0,0\rangle\]

System = ball B

\[\Delta\vec p_{sys}=\langle 45,0,0\rangle,\qquad \Delta\vec p_{surr}=\langle -45,0,0\rangle\]

System = both balls

\[\boxed{\;\Delta\vec p_{sys}=\langle 0,0,0\rangle,\qquad \Delta\vec p_{surr}=\langle 0,0,0\rangle\;}\]
Question

Two clay lumps, each \(m=0.037\ \mathrm{kg}\), collide and stick. Just before: \(\vec v_1=\langle 8,8,-5\rangle\), \(\vec v_2=\langle -5,0,-8\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\). Find (a) total momentum before, (b) momentum after, (c) the final velocity.

Worked solution

(a)/(b) Total momentum (conserved)

\[\vec p_{tot}=m(\vec v_1+\vec v_2)=0.037\langle 3,8,-13\rangle=\langle 0.111,0.296,-0.481\rangle\ \mathrm{kg\cdot m/s}\]

(c) Final velocity

\[\boxed{\;\vec v_f=\dfrac{\vec p_{tot}}{2m}=\dfrac{\langle 3,8,-13\rangle}{2}=\langle 1.5,\,4,\,-6.5\rangle\ \mathrm{m/s}\;}\]
Question

A \(4.2\ \mathrm{kg}\) block on a table. A push under \(20.6\ \mathrm{N}\) won't move it. Once moving, \(13.6\ \mathrm{N}\) keeps it at constant speed. Find \(\mu_s\) and \(\mu_k\).

Worked solution

Normal force: \(N=mg=4.2\times 9.8=41.16\ \mathrm{N}\).

Static

At the verge of slipping, push \(=\mu_s N\):

\[\boxed{\;\mu_s=\dfrac{20.6}{41.16}\approx 0.500\;}\]

Kinetic

At constant speed, applied force \(=\mu_k N\):

\[\boxed{\;\mu_k=\dfrac{13.6}{41.16}\approx 0.330\;}\]
Question

An \(8.2\ \mathrm{kg}\) box on a belt, \(\mu_s=0.8,\ \mu_k=0.6\). Find max static friction, kinetic friction, and the friction vector (plus the matching ball-spring picture) for constant speed, speeding up \(0\!\to\!0.9\ \mathrm{m/s}\) in \(2\,\mathrm s\), and slowing to a stop in \(2\,\mathrm s\).

Worked solution

Big idea: a solid isn't truly rigid — microscopically it's like jello, a lattice of atoms joined by springs. The belt's contact springs supply both the normal force (vertical squeeze) and friction (horizontal shear).

Normal force: \(N=mg=8.2\times 9.8=80.36\ \mathrm{N}\).

Friction magnitudes

\[f_{s,\max}=\mu_s N=0.8(80.36)\approx 64.3\ \mathrm{N},\qquad f_k=\mu_k N=0.6(80.36)\approx 48.2\ \mathrm{N}\]

Constant speed (no slip)

No acceleration \(\Rightarrow\ \vec f=\langle 0,0,0\rangle\ \mathrm{N}\). Springs only squeezed straight down → the symmetric picture.

Speeding up

\[a=\dfrac{0.9-0}{2}=0.45\ \mathrm{m/s^2}\ \Rightarrow\ \vec f=m\vec a=\langle 3.69,0,0\rangle\ \mathrm{N}\]

The box's inertia drags it backward relative to the belt, so the springs shear back and push the box forward → the 3rd ball-spring option.

Slowing down

\[a=\dfrac{0-0.9}{2}=-0.45\ \mathrm{m/s^2}\ \Rightarrow\ \vec f=\langle -3.69,0,0\rangle\ \mathrm{N}\]

Inertia carries the box forward, springs shear the other way, friction points back → the 1st option.

Question

A \(0.3\ \mathrm{kg}\) mass on a spring has period \(1.0\ \mathrm s\). With an unknown mass the period is \(1.5\ \mathrm s\). Find the unknown mass.

Worked solution

\[T=2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{m}{k}}\ \Rightarrow\ T\propto\sqrt{m}\ \Rightarrow\ \dfrac{T_2}{T_1}=\sqrt{\dfrac{m_2}{m_1}}\]

\[\boxed{\;m_2=m_1\left(\dfrac{T_2}{T_1}\right)^2=0.3\left(\dfrac{1.5}{1.0}\right)^2=0.675\ \mathrm{kg}\;}\]
Question

\(m=1.6\ \mathrm{kg},\ k=170\ \mathrm{N/m}\), released from rest at \(x=+0.06\ \mathrm m\) at \(t=0\). Find (a) \(x\) at \(t=0.15\ \mathrm s\), (b) the first time \(x=0.03\ \mathrm m\).

Worked solution

Released from rest at max displacement \(\Rightarrow\) cosine, amplitude \(A=0.06\ \mathrm m\):

\[\omega=\sqrt{\dfrac{k}{m}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{170}{1.6}}=10.31\ \mathrm{rad/s},\qquad x(t)=0.06\cos(\omega t)\]

(a) Position at \(t=0.15\ \mathrm s\)

\[\boxed{\;x=0.06\cos(1.546)\approx 0.00148\ \mathrm m\;}\]

(b) First time \(x=0.03\ \mathrm m\)

\[\cos(\omega t)=\tfrac12\ \Rightarrow\ \omega t=\dfrac{\pi}{3}\]

\[\boxed{\;t=\dfrac{\pi/3}{\omega}=\dfrac{1.0472}{10.31}\approx 0.102\ \mathrm s\;}\]
Question

A spring-mass system stretched \(5\ \mathrm{cm}\) oscillates with period \(2\ \mathrm s\). (a) If stretched \(6\ \mathrm{cm}\) instead, what's the period? (b) If the mass is quadrupled?

Worked solution

(a) Change amplitude

\(T=2\pi\sqrt{m/k}\) has no amplitude in it — period is independent of how far you stretch:

\[\boxed{\;T=2\ \mathrm s\;}\]

(b) Quadruple the mass

\[T\propto\sqrt{m}\ \Rightarrow\ \dfrac{T_{new}}{T_{old}}=\sqrt{\dfrac{4m}{m}}=2\]

\[\boxed{\;T_{new}=2\times 2=4\ \mathrm s\;}\]
