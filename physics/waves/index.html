Waves & Sound

Wave characteristics · interference · standing waves · boundaries · Doppler · sound & intensity
Question

A sound wave has a frequency of \(f=256\ \mathrm{Hz}\) and a wavelength of \(\lambda=1.32\ \mathrm m\).

Find (a) the speed of the wave and (b) its period.

Worked solution

(a) Speed — universal wave equation

\[v=f\lambda=256\times 1.32\]

\[\boxed{\;v\approx 338\ \mathrm{m/s}\;}\]

(b) Period

\[T=\dfrac{1}{f}=\dfrac{1}{256}\]

\[\boxed{\;T\approx 3.91\times10^{-3}\ \mathrm s\;}\]
Question

Two pulses travel toward each other on the same rope and overlap at one instant. Pulse A is an upward crest of \(+3\ \mathrm{cm}\); pulse B is \(\pm 2\ \mathrm{cm}\).

Find the resultant displacement when (a) B is an upward crest of \(+2\ \mathrm{cm}\), and (b) B is a downward trough of \(-2\ \mathrm{cm}\). Name each case.

Worked solution

Principle of superposition: where waves overlap, the resultant displacement is the sum of the individual displacements.

(a) Two crests meet

\[y=(+3)+(+2)=+5\ \mathrm{cm}\]

\[\boxed{\;+5\ \mathrm{cm\ up\ —\ \textbf{constructive interference}}\;}\]

(b) Crest meets trough

\[y=(+3)+(-2)=+1\ \mathrm{cm}\]

\[\boxed{\;+1\ \mathrm{cm\ up\ —\ \textbf{destructive interference}}\;}\]
Question

A guitar string of length \(L=1.2\ \mathrm m\) is fixed at both ends. Waves travel along it at \(v=420\ \mathrm{m/s}\).

Find (a) the wavelength of the fundamental \((n=1)\) and the third harmonic \((n=3)\), and (b) the fundamental frequency.

Worked solution

For a string fixed at both ends, each end is a node, so \(L=\dfrac{n\lambda_n}{2}\Rightarrow \lambda_n=\dfrac{2L}{n}\).

(a) Wavelengths

\[\lambda_1=\dfrac{2L}{1}=2(1.2)=2.4\ \mathrm m,\qquad \lambda_3=\dfrac{2L}{3}=\dfrac{2.4}{3}=0.8\ \mathrm m\]

(b) Fundamental frequency

\[f_1=\dfrac{v}{\lambda_1}=\dfrac{420}{2.4}\]

\[\boxed{\;f_1=175\ \mathrm{Hz}\;}\]

(Higher harmonics are whole-number multiples: \(f_n=nf_1\), so \(f_3=525\ \mathrm{Hz}\).)

Question

An upward crest travels along a rope toward its end. Describe the reflected pulse when the end is (a) tied rigidly to a wall (fixed end), and (b) attached to a light ring that slides freely on a frictionless pole (free end).

Worked solution

(a) Fixed-end reflection

The rigid wall cannot move. By Newton's third law it pushes back on the rope, so the pulse is reflected inverted:

\[\boxed{\;\text{crest}\ \rightarrow\ \text{trough (inverted)}\;}\]

(b) Free-end reflection

The free ring can move, so there is no inverting force. The pulse reflects upright:

\[\boxed{\;\text{crest}\ \rightarrow\ \text{crest (upright)}\;}\]
Question

On a day when the air temperature is \(T=24\ ^\circ\mathrm C\):

(a) Find the local speed of sound using \(v=331.4+0.606\,T\) (\(T\) in \(^\circ\mathrm C\)).

(b) A jet flies at \(412\ \mathrm{m/s}\). Find its Mach number.

Worked solution

(a) Local speed of sound

\[v=331.4+0.606(24)=331.4+14.5\]

\[\boxed{\;v\approx 345.9\ \mathrm{m/s}\;}\]

(b) Mach number

\[M=\dfrac{\text{airspeed of object}}{\text{local speed of sound}}=\dfrac{412}{345.9}\]

\[\boxed{\;M\approx 1.19\;}\]

\(M>1\), so the jet is supersonic.

Question

A train sounds a \(f_0=520\ \mathrm{Hz}\) horn while moving at \(32\ \mathrm{m/s}\) toward a stationary observer. The local speed of sound is \(v_{sound}=343\ \mathrm{m/s}\).

Find the frequency the observer hears (a) as the train approaches, and (b) after it passes. Use \(f_{obs}=\left(\dfrac{v_{sound}}{v_{sound}+v_{source}}\right)f_0\), where \(v_{source}\) is negative approaching and positive receding.

Worked solution

The observer is stationary, so \(v_{detector}=0\).

(a) Approaching \((v_{source}=-32)\)

\[f_{obs}=\left(\dfrac{343}{343+(-32)}\right)520=\dfrac{343}{311}\times 520\]

\[\boxed{\;f_{obs}\approx 573\ \mathrm{Hz}\ (\text{higher pitch})\;}\]

(b) Receding \((v_{source}=+32)\)

\[f_{obs}=\left(\dfrac{343}{343+32}\right)520=\dfrac{343}{375}\times 520\]

\[\boxed{\;f_{obs}\approx 476\ \mathrm{Hz}\ (\text{lower pitch})\;}\]
Question

A small loudspeaker radiates \(P=0.50\ \mathrm W\) of sound power uniformly in all directions. Find the sound intensity at a distance of \(r=4.0\ \mathrm m\).

Worked solution

Intensity is power per unit area, and the energy spreads over the surface of a sphere of radius \(r\):

\[I=\dfrac{P}{4\pi r^{2}}=\dfrac{0.50}{4\pi(4.0)^{2}}=\dfrac{0.50}{201.1}\]

\[\boxed{\;I\approx 2.5\times10^{-3}\ \mathrm{W/m^2}\;}\]

Because \(I\propto 1/r^{2}\), doubling the distance to \(8\ \mathrm m\) would quarter the intensity.

Question

The threshold of hearing is \(I_0=1\times10^{-12}\ \mathrm{W/m^2}\). A sound has intensity \(I=1\times10^{-5}\ \mathrm{W/m^2}\).

Find its sound level \(\beta=10\log_{10}\!\left(\dfrac{I}{I_0}\right)\) in decibels.

Worked solution

\[\dfrac{I}{I_0}=\dfrac{1\times10^{-5}}{1\times10^{-12}}=10^{7}\]

\[\beta=10\log_{10}(10^{7})=10\times 7\]

\[\boxed{\;\beta=70\ \mathrm{dB}\;}\]

Sound level (dB) is a logarithmic comparison to a reference intensity — it is not the same as the intensity itself.
